Ang Kaew Reservoir

"Ang Kaew Reservoir" located in front of Chiang Mai University. Constructed to supply water for the university, it is also a recreational area for Chiang Mai University students , campus residents and the local community.

239 Huaykaew Road, Su-thep Sub-district, Su-thep District,
Chiang Mai, Thailand, 50200

“Nice views and a good place to relax”

Reviewed February 12, 2017 by Ian B

I stopped by December 2016. I wanted to get a few photos and take in the views. There is a coffee shop so you could come along to relax and unwind for a while. I just rode straight in and parked up on my scooter and nobody seemed to mind.

“Great lake view but nothing more”

Reviewed January 22, 2017 by dukekev

Ang Kaew Reservoir is located in Chiang Mai University campus, but tourists are not allowed to access the campus by themselves. Tourists must buy the shuttle ticket near the main entrance and the shuttle will bring you around the campus with audio guide. The only stop is at Ang Kawe Reservoir which tourists have 10 minutes to take pictures before getting back to the shuttle. The lake is beautiful which you can see the water reflection. But there is nothing more and there is too little time to enjoy to view. Not a very worthwhile place to visit at all, given the restriction like this.

““Peaceful Nature at Lake Ang Kaew in CMU...””

Reviewed February 18, 2016 by Jeshua T

This is by far our favorite hangout place in Chiangmai. Short walk from CMU entrance on Huay Kaew Road - you will come to a specatacular view of the lake against Doi Suthep in the background...breathtaking! Take a walk along the right to the end, there is a quiet patch of greens where we do our morning meditation and yoga - absolutely fantastic! Go early in the morning to catch the warm morning Sun, it is really delightful. There are usually a lot of tourists in the afternoon although late afternoon/ early evenings around 430pm is also wonderful - if you don't mind the people/ tourists walking around taking pix... There is a big sign that says NO DOGS ALLOWED, despite, my lovely dog and I have been enjoying the lake (no swimming) - spreading love and joy to people we meet. My dog is a very well behaved dog - absolutely adorable so there had not been any complaints! ;-)
